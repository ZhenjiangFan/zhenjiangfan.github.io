My research focuses on elucidating complex biological interactions using and developing computational techniques such as machine learning, deep neural networks, graph neural networks, causal discovery and inference, large language models, multimodal language models, and network analysis, as well as building mobile and web applications for biological, clinical, and computational related research.

Beyond this research, I am passionate about leveraging and advancing computer vision to detect complex biological structures and activities in large-scale clinical and biomedical imaging datasets. I also explore computational methods for analyzing biological systems (Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) data (bulk RNA-seq, single-cell RNA-seq, multiomics (e.g., genomics, transcriptomics, proteomics), and spatial transcriptomics), including multimodal language models. Additionally, I am interested in developing simulation tools for biological dynamics—such as multi-scale molecular and cellular interactions—using open-source frameworks like MuJoCo.

I've been working on various research topics, such as uncovering genetic regulatory mechanisms and interactions, identifying genetic functionalities and roles in certain biological activities, discovering nuanced cell types, identifying cell-type–specific activities and events, learning cell-to-cell communications, finding critical biomarkers to accurately predict disease outcomes from clinical data, inferring causal relationships from complex biological and clinical environments, and so on.
